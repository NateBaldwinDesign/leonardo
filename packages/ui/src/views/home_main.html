Why use Leonardo?

Color science driven design

Leonardo gives designers access to more color spaces than any other tool: RGB, HSL, HSV, Lab, LCh, HSLuv, CIECAM02 (Jab), and CIECAM02 (JCh).

Interpolating in different color spaces results in unique appearances to the scale (or gradient) that is generated.

The CIECAM02 color space (Jab and JCh) is a more perceptually accurate model of how we see color. Leonardo gives you the ability to visualize and interpolate in this space to ensure your color scales are smooth and balanced.

Adaptive theming

Leonardo is the first-of-its kind adaptive theming tool. Themes created in Leonardo are dynamic and constraint-based, giving you (or your users) control over brightness, contrast, and saturation of the entire theme.

IMAGE

This makes dark-mode support, multi-theme support, or nuanced theme-wide changes to color far more manageable than any other tool out there.

Contrast-based color generation

Colors are defined in Leonardo as color scales (gradients). Theme color swatches are determined by meeting the target contrast ratios you define.

IMAGE

Since colors are generated based on target contrast ratios, you can easily increase or decrease the overall contrast of your theme.

You can also define your swatches by desired luminosity. This enables you to create perceptually uniform distribution of luminosities for all of your colors.

Designer-developer collaborative tool

Leonardo is built to be used by both designers and developers. As you make adjustments in the interface, javascript parameters and CSS variables are updated for you to share with engineers. By sharing the URL to your Leonardo theme, developers can access these outputs directly.

IMAGE

If you are implementing an adaptive theme in your application, the javascript parameter output gives you all you need to regenerate the theme within your own application.

IMAGE

Additional color tools in Leonardo

Aside from creating themes, Leonardo has:

  • Contrast checker that can calculate colors with transparency
  • Color converter (with all supported colorspaces)
  • Colorblind palette generator

Open source

Leonardo is the creation of the Adobe Spectrum design systems team, but we found higher value in sharing this with the community.

Leonardo's guiding principle has been to enable the creation of accessible color themes for design systems. Our team believes that accessibility is for everyone, and that our tool had higher purpose in helping other people and teams create accessile color themes.

To do this, we've made Leonardo fully open-source for others in the community to help shape and evolve this tool to fit their needs.
